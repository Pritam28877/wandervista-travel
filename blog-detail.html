Ladakh in 2026: The Complete Guide for First-Time Riders

From altitude acclimatisation to permits, from Pangong to Nubra — the only itinerary you'll need for a safe and unforgettable Leh-Manali highway run.

Arjun Mehta Senior Editor · 7 trips to Ladakh
18 May 2026 7 min read 12,408 views
Share

Most "Ladakh in X days" lists are written by people who landed at Leh and immediately tried to drive to Pangong. Don't be that person. This guide is built from seven trips, twelve cancelled plans, and one altitude-sickness scare that ended a friend's holiday on day two.

When to go

The road season runs roughly mid-May to mid-October. If you want both Manali–Leh and Srinagar–Leh open, aim for late June to early September. July has the most consistent weather but the highest tourist numbers.

  • May: Pangong is iced over in early May. Some passes still closed.
  • June–Aug: Peak season. Everything open, everything booked.
  • September: Quiet, cool, gorgeous light. Best time to ride if you can flex dates.
  • October onwards: Highway closures begin. Fly-only.

Acclimatise. Properly.

Leh sits at 3,500m. Even if you feel fine off the plane, your body is not. Spend at least 48 hours doing nothing strenuous — walk the market, eat thukpa, drink water. Skip alcohol completely for the first three days.

“Half the bad Ladakh stories I've heard start with someone landing at Leh and driving to Khardung La the next morning. Give your blood a minute.” — Dr. Rinchen Dolma, Sonam Norboo Hospital, Leh

A safe 9-day itinerary

This is the most-tested itinerary I run for first-timers. It builds altitude gradually and keeps drive days under 6 hours.

Day 1

Arrive Leh. Hotel. Sleep early.

Day 2

Acclimatisation. Local sightseeing (low effort).

Day 3

Leh → Nubra via Khardung La. Camel ride at Hunder.

Day 4

Nubra → Pangong via Shyok. Lakeside camp.

Day 5

Pangong → Leh via Chang La. Rest.

Day 6

Leh → Lamayuru → Alchi → back to Leh.

Day 7

Tso Moriri overnight (the quiet lake).

Day 8

Tso Moriri → Leh.

Day 9

Buffer / departure.

Inner Line Permits

Nubra, Pangong and Tso Moriri all need ILPs. They're free for Indian citizens but mandatory. Apply at the Leh DC office or online — keep five printed copies; checkpoints will keep one each.

Royal Enfield 411 vs Himalayan vs… anything else

The honest answer most guides won't give you: get a Himalayan. The 411 is iconic but punishing on the spine after eight hours. The Himalayan's geometry and longer travel suspension turn a survival exercise into an actual ride.

2026 cost breakdown

  • Bike rental (Himalayan, 9 days): ₹18,000–22,000
  • Fuel (full circuit): ~₹6,000
  • Stays (mix camps + guesthouses): ~₹14,000
  • Food: ~₹4,500
  • Permits + misc: ~₹1,500

One last thing

Ladakh is not a checklist. The locals who run the camps you'll stay in are some of the most welcoming people you'll meet anywhere — slow down, eat with them, leave their land cleaner than you found it. Carry your trash out. The lakes deserve it.
